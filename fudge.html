How to Make Fudge

This is my recipe for making the perfect fudge. It is the result of much research and experimentation. The secret to making good fudge is controlling the crystallisation of the sugar. Fudge-making has more in common with making candy than it does baking a cake. Good fudge is extremely smooth and will melt in your mouth (i.e. at body temperature).

You will need:

  • 3 Tbsp cocoa
  • 35g butter
  • ¾ cup milk
  • 1 tsp vanilla essence
  • A candy thermometer
  • A heavy bottom pan
  • A wooden spoon
  • A baking tin or brownie dish
  1. Lightly grease and line the tin with baking paper.
  2. Add the sugar, cocoa, butter and milk to the heavy bottom saucepan. Mix to combine.
  3. Heat gently, stirring constantly until the butter has melted and the sugar dissolved. The fudge will almost double in size while boiling. So, make sure the pot is big enough. The heavy bottom is important to stop the fudge burning.

  4. Stop stirring and bring to the boil.

    If you stir the fudge while it is boiling, you will cause large crystals to form and the fudge wil be gritty.

  5. Boil until the fudge reaches exactly 114˚C (softball stage). The melting point of fudge (and all sugar confectionary) is determined by the water content and by extension the temperature it was boiled to. By boiling the fudge to 114˚C the fudge will melt at body temperature (i.e. in your mouth). This does mean the fudge will need to be keep in the fridge to prevent it from melting at room temperature. The longer you boil the fudge the firmer it will be and the higher its melting point.

  6. Once the fudge reaches 114˚C immediately remove the pan from the heat, and add the vanilla to the pot.

  7. Let cool until it reaches 70˚C.

    Ideally you would wait until the fudge reaches 60˚C. But in the home kitchen the fudge at the outside of the pot will cool much faster than the middle. Thus, the fudge will set on the outside of the pot before the centre is cool enough. So, we cool to 70˚C as a compromise between smoothness of fudge and practically.

    The reason we wait for the fudge to cool before string is to control the crystallisation of the sugar. The sooner you stir the fudge big the crystals will be and the grainier the fudge will be.

  8. Beat vigorously with a wooden spoon until the fudge just begins to thicken. This will take a couple of minutes. As soon as the fudge begins to thicken quickly pour it into the tin. You can tell the fudge is about to thicken because it will lose its sheen. Be careful here, the fudge will set in the pot if you wait too long. So, on your first attempt you may end up with a solid block of fudge (it will still be delicious).

    By beating the fudge we force the crystallisation to occur. If you do not beat the fudge, it will be a solid lump and not have the texture you'd expect.

  9. Score the top of the fudge into squares and then cut when cold.

    Store the fudge in an airtight container in the fridge. The fudge will survive serial hours at room temperature before melting. It will survive longer the cooler it is and the higher the temperature you boiled it to.

Me Pēhea te Tunu Fudge

Ko te hurahura me te whaka-mātau me te aha taku tohu-taka mō fudge. Ko te muna o fudge i te tioa-ta. He iti tioata te fudge pai ā ka rewa ki roto o te waha.

Ka whai koe:

  • He e toru kapu o te huka
  • he e whā tēpu pūnu o te kōkō
  • he e toru tekau ma rima karamu o te pata,
  • he e toru huawhā o te kapu o te miraka,
  • he e tahi tīpune o te wanira,
  • he tētahi huka ine-mahana,
  • he tētahi toimaha kōhua,
  • he tētahi kape,
  • me tētahi paeumu pāpaku.

Uhia te paeumu ki te rautunu.

Ma-kaia roto te huka, te kōkō, te pata, me te miraka i te raro taimaha kōhua.

Ko hira te raro taimaha.

Whaka-hanumia te kai.

Whaka-maha-natia āta te kōhua, ā kōro-ria te kai kia rewa te papa me te huka.

Kāti kōrori ā whaka-maha-natia te kai ki te hūnga.

Tunua āta kia ko te kai rau tekau ma whā takiri tohurau.

Mēnā ka kōrori koe, ka kino te fugde.

Mēnā Kāore koe e tunu i fudge ki te rau tekau ma whā takiri tohurau, ka kino te fugde.

Tango-hia atu i te tāra-hu, kātahi ka maka-ia te wa-nira ki te kōhua.

Tukuna kia mātao haere, kia ono tekau takiri tohu-rau.

Mēnā kāore koe e tari-tari, ka kino te fugde.

Kaurori te kai, kia ti-mata rawa te mato-toru, kātahi ka tere tā-horo-tia ki roto i te paeumu.

Maka-tia ki nga tapa-whā rite.

Kia mā-tao te fudge, ka tope.
